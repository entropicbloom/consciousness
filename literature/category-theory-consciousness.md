# Category Theory Approaches to Consciousness

Key players: Nao Tsuchiya (Monash), Hayato Saigo (Nagahama Institute), Steven Phillips (AIST), Ryota Kanai (Araya).

---

## Tsuchiya & Taguchi (2016)
**Using category theory to assess the relationship between consciousness and integrated information theory**
*Neuroscience Research* | [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0168010215002989) | [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26748074/)

Founding paper of this line of work. Proposes breaking IIT into three categorical subprojects: (1) characterise phenomenal structure as a category, (2) characterise integrated information as a category, (3) assess whether a structure-preserving functor exists between them. If it does, hard questions about consciousness could be resolved mathematically.

## Northoff & Tsuchiya (2019)
**Mathematics and the Brain: A Category Theoretical Approach to Go Beyond the Neural Correlates of Consciousness**
*Frontiers in Psychology* | [Semantic Scholar](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/Mathematics-and-the-Brain:-A-Category-Theoretical-Northoff-Tsuchiya/b6ac33e0e44fd3dd34e6bc99f62305435b7a2742)

Argues that the NCC project is fundamentally limited and that category theory provides tools to go beyond correlation — mapping structure between brain and phenomenal domains via functors rather than just identifying co-varying neural signatures.

## Tsuchiya & Saigo (2021)
**A relational approach to consciousness: categories of level and contents of consciousness**
*Neuroscience of Consciousness* | [Oxford Academic](https://academic.oup.com/nc/article/2021/2/niab034/6397521) | [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8517618/)

Defines a "category of consciousness" where objects are conscious states and morphisms encode similarity/subsumption relations. Uses the **Yoneda lemma** to argue that a conscious state is fully characterised by all its relations to other states — not by intrinsic properties. Introduces enriched categories to handle graded similarity. Separates the project into a "category of the brain" and a "category of consciousness" to be related in a later step.

## Tsuchiya, Saigo & Phillips (2022)
**An adjunction hypothesis between qualia and reports**
*Frontiers in Psychology* | [Full text](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2022.1053977/full) | [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10107370/)

Proposes that the relationship between qualia and verbal/behavioural reports is an **adjunction** — a weaker, more flexible correspondence than an isomorphism. The left adjoint maps reports to qualia; the right adjoint maps qualia to reports. This formalises why introspective reports are systematically related to experience but never perfectly faithful. Ryota Kanai (Araya) contributed as editor.

## Tsuchiya et al. (2024)
**Category theory in consciousness science: going beyond the correlational project**
*Synthese* | [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11229-024-04718-5)

Most recent synthesis. Reviews the categorical programme and argues it enables a structural rather than merely correlational science of consciousness. Discusses how functors, natural transformations, and adjunctions operationalise theoretical claims in IIT, Global Workspace, and relational approaches.
